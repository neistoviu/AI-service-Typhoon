Typhoon Roasters — Extended Context
(for AI)
0) Product compass (what we make)
● What: Fully-electric, 100% convection coffee roasters + control software + auxiliary
equipment (cyclone, loader, destoner, filters, mixers).
● Why it matters: cleaner cup (no scorching/smoke defects), repeatability, high
throughput (up to 6–7 batches/hour, no cooldown), low energy per kg (≈0.3
kWh/kg), safer no gas/open flame, transparent chamber for live control & guest
experience.
● Service: 24-month warranty, remote diagnostics/updates, onboarding & training.
1) Segment A — Established roasters outgrowing
current setup
Who: Owners/co-founders, Head Roasters, Ops leads in small/mid roasteries and factories
(US/EU/LatAm/MENA). Budgets 30–150k€+.
Why they buy (JTBD):
● Increase hourly output without building a second line.
● Eliminate defects and lock flavor at scale.
● Cut OPEX and reduce reliance on “star” operators.
Typical triggers: backlog, flavor inconsistency at higher volumes, burner/service
headaches, gas permitting issues, staffing constraints.
Buying criteria: kg/hour, repeatability, energy/kilo, TCO (3 years), remote support,
warranty, software integration.
Common doubts: “Electric won’t keep up.” “We trust drums.” “Brand familiarity.”
What Typhoon solves:
● Up to 6–7 batches/hour with no between-batch protocol → more volume in the
same shift.
● ≈0.3 kWh/kg with hot-air recirculation → lower cost per kg.
● Smart repeat (by temperature curve, control points, parameters) → stable cup
profile.
● Fully electric → easier compliance, fewer high-temp service risks, remote updates.
Message: “Double your hourly throughput and lock your flavor—this is margin and
growth you can bank on.”
Qualify with: current vs target volume, gas/electric constraints, energy cost, decision
team, upgrade timing.

2) Segment B — Café chains & flagships roasting
in-house
Who: Owners/CEOs of café groups, Directors of Coffee.
Why they buy:
● Turn roasting into a guest experience (transparent chamber) and brand story.
● Control flavor & freshness onsite, reduce reliance on external suppliers.
Triggers: new flagship, need for differentiation, logistics pain.
Criteria: safety (no open flame), clean/quiet, easy training, visual appeal, low
odor/smoke, compact footprint.
Doubts: “Training is hard.” “Permits/exhaust scare us.”
What Typhoon solves:
● Electric & convection → simpler permitting, safer on floor.
● See-through process → marketing + higher ticket.
● Intuitive software → rapid training, repeatable results.
Message: “Roast where you serve—safe, clean, repeatable, and great for
storytelling.”
Qualify with: location & footprint, ventilation, who roasts (role), training plan, peak
hours.
3) Segment C — Newcomer startups (no prior roasting
background)
Who: Entrepreneurs/SMBs launching private-label or new roasteries.
Why they buy:
● Turn-key start without years of tribal knowledge.
● Predictable ROI with low cost per kg and repeatable quality.
Triggers: capturing margin in-house, local demand signals, white-label opportunities.
Criteria: onboarding & training, remote service, no-gas permitting, TCO.
Doubts: “Can we run it?” “How many people do we need?”
What Typhoon solves:
● Full package: equipment + software + training + remote support.
● No gas → often simpler approvals/insurance.
● Repeatability → lowers skill barrier for operators.
Message: “Start roasting right—safe, repeatable, and ROI you can model.”
Qualify with: power availability, premises/venting, go-to-market plan, planned
volumes & pricing.

4) Additional micro-segments
● Industrial/co-packers (20–30 kg): scale stability, defect reduction, energy efficiency,
food-safe process control.
● Distributors/showrooms: innovation portfolio, regional exclusivity, demo-driven lead
gen.
5) Job → Feature → Outcome (universal map)
● Increase output → up to 6–7 batches/hour, no cooldown → more kg/hour in same
labor window.
● Eliminate defects → 100% convection, clean thermodynamics → cleaner cup, fewer
returns.
● Ensure repeatability → smart repeat (temp/points/params) → predictable profiles &
contracts.
● Lower OPEX → ≈0.3 kWh/kg, recirculation, fewer high-temp service points → lower
cost per kg.
● Train faster → intuitive UI + presets → quicker ramp, less dependency on a single
guru.
● Create guest value → transparent chamber, clean process → brand distinctiveness
& higher engagement.
6) Objections & crisp replies
● “Gas is cheaper.” → Your profit is €/kg and kg/hour. Convection gives you higher
hourly throughput and no pauses—labor and ROI win.
● “Electric won’t keep up.” → Real-world energy ≈0.3 kWh/kg; throughput is up to
6–7 batches/hour with automated repeats.
● “We’re drum loyalists.” → Different physics: pure convection means fewer
scorching/smoke defects and far tighter repeatability, especially at scale.
● “Permits worry us.” → No open flame simplifies approvals and safety; remote
diagnostics reduce downtime.
● “We lack skilled people.” → Software, presets, and training reduce skill barriers
and stabilize outcomes.
7) What to include in TCO (3-year lens)

● CAPEX (what’s included by default—e.g., cyclone, software—vs add-ons with
others).
● Energy per kg (≈0.3 kWh/kg reference for shop series).
● Labor per volume (6–7 batches/h, no between-batch protocol).
● Downtime/service (burners/high temps vs electric elements & remote support).
● Defect rate/returns (clean convection = fewer defects).
● Warranty & remote updates (risk reduction, faster recovery).
8) Buying roles & what each cares about
● Founder/Owner/CEO: ROI, scalability, brand differentiation.
● Head Roaster/QA: repeatability, flavor control, no defects.
● Ops/Facility: permits, safety, infrastructure, downtime.
● Finance: 3-year TCO, €/kg, payback period.
Match messages:
● CEO/Finance → TCO, €/kg, payback.
● Roaster → thermodynamics, software repeat, visibility.
● Ops → electric safety, venting, service, remote updates.

10) Fast qualification questions (script)
1. Current weekly kg and 12-month target?
2. Where will it sit (shop floor/production)? Any gas/electric/venting constraints?
3. Your top risk today: flavor stability, hourly capacity, or €/kg?
4. Who will operate it (role) and how many staff?
5. Launch timeline and budget range?
