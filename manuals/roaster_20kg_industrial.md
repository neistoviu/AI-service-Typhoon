Typhoon 20 kg consists of a frame with a heat exchanger, a high pressure fan (main motor) and
a coffee roasting chamber. The product enters the roasting chamber at the back of which the
filter grid is installed. The pressure of the heated air creates boiling beds in which the product is
roasted. The size and location of the holes in the air distribution grid ensures the best rotation of
the product during roasting. After unloading, the product is dropped from the roasting chamber
into the cooling tray.

Preparing to start roasting
Workplace requirements
1. The working area for the roaster shall be at least 50 m2.
2. Maintain a minimum of 1 meter of free space on all sides of the equipment (Fig. 4).
3. The ceiling height must be at least 3,5 meters to allow proper installation and ventilation.
4. Smoke detection and fire suppression systems must be installed in the roasting room.
5. A powder fire extinguisher must be mounted on the wall near the roaster and clearly
marked.
Caution: Receiving the shipment will require two assistants, as well as a forklift and a pallet jack
(with a minimum fork length of 2240 mm), both rated for the weight of the crate. Pick the
machine up from the electrical cabinet side only!
Coffee Roaster installation
Caution: The roaster must be installed and operated only by a specialist trained in the safe
operation and handling of this equipment. Upon completion of the training, a certificate will be
issued authorizing the operator to use the machine.
To reduce noise and vibration, please make sure to:
1. Ensure an airtight connection between the ventilation system and the roaster’s air-duct
outlets (smoke extractor and cooling tray).
2. Check the equipment for any foreign objects that may obstruct the movement of
mechanical parts.
3. Inspect all mechanical connections and tighten any loose screws or fasteners.
4. Tighten all contact terminals inside the roaster’s electrical cabinet ( the diagram is
included in the accessory case supplied with the roaster).
5. Connect the roaster to the power supply.
6. Verify the phase sequence and correct it if necessary.
7. If needed, apply acoustic foam to the wall to reduce noise levels.
Caution: Proper operation of the roasting equipment, along with timely removal of chaff, helps
prevent the risk of ignition.

Electricity supply connection.
A licensed professional must install or evaluate the power supply at the installation site,
according to the specifications provided in this document.
Connect the roaster to a 98A/380V power socket. The standard equipment package includes:
- Portable power plug (3 m cable, male plug)
- Stationary power socket (female socket) for installation in the roasting room
The required electricity power sufficient for uninterrupted operation of the equipment must be
at least 67 kW. Before putting the equipment into operation, all wire terminals must be fully
tightened up. After 150 - 200 hours of operation, the terminals must be tightened up again.
Please pay special attention to the terminals on electric heating elements and electric motors.
After connection, ensure that the fan and other drives rotate in the correct direction.
Ventilation connection
1. The exhaust piping must be as short as possible and installed with an upward slope of at
least 12°. Sharp bends and reductions in diameter should be avoided.
2. The exhaust ducts from the roasting chamber and cooling tray must not exceed 10
meters in length and should include no more than two 90° bends. If this is not
achievable, an additional exhaust fan must be installed in the system.
3. Easily accessible cleaning openings and a drain valve for condensate that may
accumulate inside the piping are recommended.
4. The roaster emits a significant volume of exhaust gases into the atmosphere. It is
essential to ensure sufficient room ventilation and an adequate air supply from
outside.
5. It is recommended to inspect the exhaust ducts for residue buildup every 150 hours of
operation.
6. The exhaust air outlet from the roaster must be directed vertically above the roof level.
The chimney must be properly protected against rain and snow.
7. All points where the exhaust pipe passes through the roof or walls must be fireproofed
in accordance with the exhaust ventilation system requirements.
8. Local building and fire safety regulations must be taken into account when designing
and installing the chimney.
9. In case of any uncertainty, please contact an authorized Typhoon technician.
10. The operator is responsible for the correct operation and maintenance of the exhaust
and chimney system.
Note: A small amount of smoke may be released during the bean discharge process.
This smoke should be removed either by a local extraction hood or by general
room ventilation.

Warnings about the ways in which equipment and
mechanisms shall not be used
1. It is not recommended to start electric air heaters without running the main motor;
2. It is not recommended to operate the roaster without connecting to the ventilation
system which includes smoke extraction pipes both from the roast chamber and the
cooling tray.
3. It is not recommended to use the equipment in case of heavy contamination of the
internal surfaces such as: roasting camera, chaff collecting and sifting container, section
where electric air heaters are installed, Main Motor tube.
4. It is not recommended to use the equipment in case of particularly strong vibration or
extraneous noise coming from the electric cabinet and Main Motor work;
5. It is not recommended to forcibly pull out the roast chamber with coffee if for some
reason it comes out with difficulty - there is a risk of damage to the telescopic guides,
which are not included to the warranty parts servicing;
6. It is not recommended to use the equipment set on the transportation metal frame as
this may cause lack of access to the equipment parts as well as increased vibration of
the roaster frame and, as a result, damage to the elements of the electric cabinet.
Improper operation prohibitions
1. Do not use equipment with the roaster door and side panels (Fig. 5) open;
1. Do not use the "Cooling Tray Blades" function if there are any foreign objects in the
cooling tray.
2. Do not use the equipment if any component is not connected properly;
3. Do not use the equipment if the electricity has not been grounded or neutralized;
4. Do not use the equipment if any part of it is not functioning properly;
5. Do not use the coffee roaster for other purposes (e.g. roasting nuts, seeds, etc.);
6. Do not use the equipment that has not been set to the level of the roasting camera;
7. Do not use the equipment if the circuit cable or other electric elements have been
damaged;
8. Do not use the equipment if chaff container is filled with chaff;
9. Do not use the equipment or activate electric air heaters when the main motor fan is
not working or running on less than 50% of its capacity;
10.Do not use the equipment if air intake is hindered in any of fans (i.e., there is a bag or a
rag on the protective case of the motor that interferes with air intake) or a motor is too
close to the wall;
11.Do not use the equipment which is in a dirty condition; damage may occur to such
elements as the roasting camera, temperature thermocouples, electric air heaters and
the main motor.
12.Do not apply high physical mechanical force on the roast chamber;
13. Do not turn the motor off if the heater element temperature is above 180 degrees. The
roaster should be cooled in "service mode" using the "motor" button before leaving it
turned off at the end of the day.

Instructions for taking preventive actions
1. All electric connections must be tightened up once in 150 of roast hours;
(This procedure should be performed by a professional with knowledge of electrical and
instrumentation engineering and trained in electrical safety for equipment up to 1000 Watts);
2. Adjust door hermetic sealing once in 600 roast hours;
3. Check the equipment leveling once in 500 roast hours;
4. Check up the fixing bolts of the smoke exhaust pipe once in 300 roast hours;
5. Perform cleaning of coffee dust and oil from the inner parts surfaces once in 24 roast hours;
6. Perform thorough cleaning of the inner surfaces once in 150 roast hours;
7. Perform cleaning of the cooling tray once in 350 roast hours;
8. Check cleanness of the ventilation tubes, change or clean them once in 3000 roast hours;

Coffee roasting instructions
1. Connect the main cable to the power grid.
2. Switch on the main circuit breaker;
3. Turn the lighting in the roast chamber on;
4. Select the roast profile using the touch-screen of the console;
5. In Home tab set the necessary temperature of heating;
6. Warm up for 20 - 40 minutes. To start the process, turn switcher M clockwise. The
loading temperature depends on the type of coffee and can vary from 150 °C to 200 °С.
Make sure that the cyclone is running.
7. Load the raw coffee batch to the hopper;
8. Wait until the temperature drops down to the pre-set in the window “Bean temp” value;
9. Push the green button and wait until the Main Motor will reduce its revolution rate to
50% or less of its capacity;
10. Rotate the handle of the hopper counterclockwise (from console) all the way;
11. Push the green button once more (coffee starts roasting);
12. When you want to finish the roast, press the red Stop button;
13. Lift the chamber weight and pull the roast chamber using its handle;
14. Wait when the roasted coffee is cooled (2 - 3 minutes);
15. Push the button to activate cooling tray fan to stop cooling;
16. Activate the cooling table blades and open the valve of the cooling tray to unload the
coffee;
17. Turn the switcher M to the position M turning it counterclockwise;
18. Wait when the Main Motor stops to proceed with cleaning the chaff container;
19. Put the chaff container in its initial position in the roaster, turn the switcher M clockwise
for the preparation/heating mode;
Coffee roaster is ready for the next batch.

Fire safety precautions and what to do in case of inflammation
In the room where the coffee roaster is placed, it is necessary to have: fire extinguisher with a
dry mixture (such fire extinguishers do not cause deformation of metal by its rapid cooling, but
simply put out the fire)
To prevent a fire from starting, it is necessary:
1. Timely cleaning chaff from the chaff container
2. Do not exceed the temperature of the coffee roaster over 280 °C;
3. Do not allow chaff to get into the chamber with the electric air heaters (check the purity
of the lower chamber after 10 roasts).
4. In case of chaff inflammation, it is necessary:
5. Unload the product into the chamber of the cooling tray as soon as possible;
6. Close any air supply and open the exhaust outlet;
7. Press the emergency button (to stop the roast cycle), then unscrew the emergency
