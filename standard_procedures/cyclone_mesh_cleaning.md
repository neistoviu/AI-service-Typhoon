# Standard Procedure: Cyclone Mesh Cleaning

> **When to use:** Airflow loss, coffee not flying in chamber, temperature loss  
> **Frequency:** Check every 150 operating hours or when airflow drops

## Why This Matters

A dirty/clogged cyclone mesh is the **#1 cause of airflow loss**. Reduced airflow leads to:
- Coffee not creating fluidized bed
- Uneven roasting
- Temperature loss
- Longer roast times

## Steps

1. Let roaster cool down completely
2. Access the cyclone mesh/filter grid (located between main motor and roasting chamber)
3. Remove the mesh
4. Clean thoroughly — remove all chaff, residue, and oil buildup
5. Inspect mesh for damage (holes, bent sections)
6. Replace mesh if damaged
7. Reinstall securely
8. Verify airflow by running a test roast

## Frequency

- **After every 5 roasts:** Remove chaff from chaff collector bucket
- **Every 150 operating hours:** Full mesh cleaning
- **Immediately** when airflow drops or coffee stops flying

## Recurring Problem?

If mesh clogs repeatedly (within a few batches), also check:
- Cyclone motor socket (may be periodically disconnecting — case TRE-261)
- Seal under motor (collapsed seal forces more chaff through mesh)
- Motor phasing (incorrect phasing reduces airflow effectiveness)
