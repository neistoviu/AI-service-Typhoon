# Standard Procedure: Heater Resistance Check

> **When to use:** Temperature loss, heater not working, repeated burnout  
> **Required tool:** Multimeter (resistance/ohm mode)  
> **Safety:** Disconnect roaster from power supply before testing!

## Steps

1. **Disconnect** the roaster from the power supply
2. Open the electrical cabinet
3. Locate the heater wiring connections
4. Set multimeter to **resistance mode** (Ω)
5. Measure resistance of **each heating element** individually
6. Compare readings:
   - **Normal:** Consistent resistance across all elements (refer to model specs)
   - **Faulty:** Open circuit (∞ / OL) = broken element
   - **Faulty:** Very low resistance = short circuit in element
7. Identify which heating element is faulty
8. Replace faulty element(s)

## After Replacement

- Verify all connections are tight
- Check relay condition (leaking relay causes repeated burnout)
- Measure voltage across phases under load to detect neutral shift
