# Standard Procedure: Front Part Alignment (Roaster Leveling)

> **When to use:** Smoke from plate area, gaps at rear, seal compression issues  
> **Video instruction:** ⚠️ Link to be added (referenced in service cases as "video for front part alignment")

## When This Is Needed

- Smoke coming from back/plate area of roaster
- Gaps found at the rear of roasting chamber
- Lower sensor error due to overheating (gap at back)
- After seal replacement if problem persists

## Steps

1. **Remove spacers** from the front of the roaster
2. **Realign the front part** following the video instruction
3. Check that the plate sits flush with no gaps:
   - Left side
   - Right side
   - Top
   - Bottom
   - **Especially the rear** (most common gap location)
4. Verify all seals are properly seated
5. **Tighten latches** — consider replacing with longer latches (extended thread) if needed
6. Test by running a roast and checking for smoke/air leaks

## Case Reference

David Pennington (20 electro): Found **1.5 cm gap at the back** that client hadn't noticed. After realignment per video instruction → problem resolved.
