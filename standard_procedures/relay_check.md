# Standard Procedure: Relay Check

> **When to use:** Repeated heater burnout, intermittent temperature loss  
> **Required tool:** Multimeter  
> **Safety:** Disconnect roaster from power supply before testing!

## Why Check Relays?

If a relay "leaks" current (passes current when it should be off), it causes:
- Gradual heater degradation
- Repeated heater burnout
- Inconsistent temperature control

## Steps

1. **Disconnect** the roaster from power supply
2. Open electrical cabinet
3. Locate the heater relays
4. Check relay condition:
   - Visual inspection for burn marks, discoloration
   - Measure continuity when relay should be OPEN (should show ∞)
   - If relay passes current when off → **relay is faulty**
5. Replace faulty relay with correct rating

## Important

- On Typhoon 5 kg: KM1 module sometimes shipped as **18A instead of 25A** — always verify the rating
- After relay replacement, also check heaters (relay leak may have caused damage)
